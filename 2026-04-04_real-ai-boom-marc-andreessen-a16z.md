## Key Takeaways

- **Demographic timing:** Demographic decline creates labor scarcity across the West and China. AI augments remaining workers, making them more valuable rather than dispensable.

- **Task versus job distinction:** Jobs comprise bundles of tasks. As individual tasks automate, the bundle shifts while the overall job persists longer than its component parts.

- **E-shaped advantage:** Combining genuine depth in one area with real competency across two or three adjacent fields generates multiplicative value difficult to commoditize.

- **Non-fungibility matters:** Developing distinctive skill combinations and perspectives makes workers irreplaceable.

- **Bloom's two-sigma effect:** One-on-one tutoring elevates students from 50th to 98th percentile performance. AI democratizes this breakthrough globally.

- **Model commoditization:** DeepSeek demonstrated that major AI advances rapidly become reimplementable. Competitive advantages shift toward distribution, trust, and product experience.

- **Regulatory constraints:** Physical-world progress suffers from regulatory barriers, validating Peter Thiel's "bits versus atoms" critique.

- **One-person companies:** Founders orchestrating autonomous AI agents at scale transitions from theoretical to plausible.
